## Executive summary

**The mathematics is sound. The evidence chain is not.**

The good news, verified empirically and independently: the cube model matches Kociemba's published CubieCube tables exactly and survives a hand-derived facelet simulator cross-check; every coordinate encode/decode is a verified bijection; the cached move/pruning tables are byte-identical to fresh rebuilds; the corner PDB reproduces Korf's published distribution (max 11, mean ~8.764); **no admissibility violation was found anywhere** — h(s) <= d*(s) held on every probe for every heuristic; the 16-symmetry phase-1 table verifies 0-mismatch against the raw BFS table (959,761,462 entries, reproduced live in ~18 s); and the superflip = 20 proof chain replays correctly. The three brief goals (G1, G2, G3) are all substantively met.

The dominant failure mode — accounting for the critical finding and most majors — is **claims/evidence drift**: the thesis text and the frozen result artifacts describe an *older* version of the code than what is in the repository. A supervisor who re-runs the documented commands will get numbers that contradict the printed tables, a failing audit gate, and a failing test suite at step 1 of Appendix A. None of this is academic dishonesty — statuses are honestly labeled throughout — but it is exactly the kind of thing a strict supervisor will find in the first hour.

The five clusters, in fix-priority order:

1. **Thesis describes code that no longer exists (CRITICAL).** Chapters 03/04/05/07 describe a bounded-IDA* Thistlethwaite with candidate collection and timeouts; the committed solver is a rewritten table-guided greedy descent with neither. Chapter 07 even proposes as *future work* what the code already does. The saved Thistlethwaite benchmark rows came from the old solver and do not reproduce — one published number (max length 27) is exceeded by the current code (32).
2. **Frozen evidence vs. current defaults.** The new 7-edge PDBs are silently included by default (`use_seven_edge_pdbs=True`), so every frozen Korf evidence artifact (all rows: `table_size_bytes=428,803,832`) no longer reproduces. The e2e artifact and its thesis table are stale (published 4 vs. rerun 3; lower bound 7 vs. 9). All 46 canonical artifacts are tagged `no_commit+dirty` — no thesis number ties to a committed revision.
3. **Reproduction gates fail in the examiner's hands.** `scripts/thesis_audit.py` fails its own implementation acceptance (six scripts moved to `scripts/experimental/`), and `python -m pytest -q` fails at HEAD (one stale assertion in the portfolio-oracle test) — the two commands Appendix A lists first.
4. **Exactness provenance gaps.** ~96 % of the exact-certificate store inherits `status="exact", verified=True` from third-party nissy benchmark *file labels* with no local optimality proof, and the caveat is erased downstream. `--root-move-mask` lets the native solver emit `status="exact"` for provably non-optimal solutions. Two thesis-cited validation numbers (the 959,761,462/0 sym-table gate; the nissy superflip 120 s/240 s timeouts) have **no saved artifact** — violating the repo's own integrity rule (both reproduce when re-run; they were simply never persisted).
5. **Benchmark methodology confounds.** The headline h48 speedups (189.894x, 38.198x, 40.16x) are dominated by cold-vs-warm page-cache ordering, not the claimed per-process overhead — steady-state ratios are ~10x, ~4x, ~11x. All are single ordered runs (n=1) quoted to 6 decimals. Separately, the flagship scoped Kociemba entry point cannot solve typical random 18–20-move scrambles under its documented defaults (phase-1 starves phase-2; `phase1_max_depth=10` < diameter 12).

Also worth knowing: the test suite's live optimality verification tops out near distance 8 (Python path: depth 4), the shipped multi-GB PDB binaries have no integrity/distribution test, and the "oracle contract" tests certify via source-code substring greps rather than behavior.
